Physics is the study of matter, energy, forces, and how they interact in the universe.

Motion describes how an object changes position over time.

Speed is the distance traveled per unit of time.

Velocity is speed with a direction.

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity over time.

Force is a push or pull that can change an object's motion.

Newton's First Law states that an object at rest stays at rest and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by a force.

Newton's Second Law states that force equals mass times acceleration.

Newton's Third Law states that every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

Mass is the amount of matter in an object.

Weight is the force of gravity acting on an object.

Gravity is a force that attracts objects toward each other, especially toward Earth.

Work is done when a force moves an object over a distance.

Energy is the ability to do work.

Kinetic energy is the energy of motion.

Potential energy is stored energy due to an object's position or state.

The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.

Power is the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred.

Momentum is mass times velocity.

Friction is a force that opposes motion between two surfaces in contact.

Friction converts kinetic energy into heat energy.

Waves transfer energy without transferring matter.

Sound waves require a medium like air or water to travel.

Light waves can travel through a vacuum and do not require a medium.

Electric current is the flow of electric charge.

Voltage is the electrical potential difference between two points.

Resistance is a measure of how much a material opposes electric current.

Ohm's law states that voltage equals current times resistance.
